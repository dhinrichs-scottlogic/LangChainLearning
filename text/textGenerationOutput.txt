Abstract art has a rich and diverse history that spans thousands of years, with early examples of non-representational art found in ancient civilizations such as Egypt, Mesopotamia, and Africa.
In the early 20th century, abstract art began to emerge as a distinct movement, with artists such as Wassily Kandinsky, Kazimir Malevich, and Robert Delaunay creating works that emphasized color, shape, and form over representational content.
